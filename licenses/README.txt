THE FOUR LICENSES

1. OML-F (FREE/OPEN SOURCE LICENSE)
   File: OML-F.txt
   
   Truly open source music licensing with no restrictions.
   
   • Completely FREE - no revenue limits ever
   • Can modify, sell, redistribute freely
   • Can extract and use stems/samples
   • Can create derivative works and sample packs
   • Only requires: attribution + license inclusion
   • Similar to MIT/Apache licenses for software
   
   Best for: Creators who believe in open culture and maximum sharing

2. OML-P (PERSONAL USE LICENSE)
   File: OML-P.txt
   
   Free for small-scale and emerging creators.
   
   • FREE for projects under $1,000/year revenue
   • Can monetize on streaming, YouTube, social media
   • Requires attribution
   • Must upgrade to OML-C when exceeding $1K
   • Compatible with sample library licenses (Splice, etc.)
   
   Best for: Supporting emerging artists while reserving commercial rights

3. OML-C (COMMERCIAL USE LICENSE)
   File: OML-C.txt
   
   For unlimited commercial use with negotiated compensation.
   
   • Required when revenue exceeds $1,000/year
   • Negotiated payment: royalties, flat fee, or hybrid
   • No revenue limits or restrictions
   • Quarterly reporting for royalty structures
   • Compatible with sample library licenses (Splice, etc.)
   
   Best for: Professional commercial projects and established artists

4. OML-S (SYNC LICENSE)
   File: OML-S.txt
   
   For synchronization with visual media.
   
   • Flat fee based on project scope
   • Covers both master + sync rights in one agreement
   • Specify: term, territory, media, usage
   • File cue sheets with PROs
   • Project-specific licensing
   
   Best for: Film, TV, advertising, video games, branded content

CHOOSING THE RIGHT LICENSE

AS A CREATOR:

Choose OML-F when:
• You want maximum openness and sharing
• You don't need revenue from the music
• You support open source philosophy
• You want to build community around your work

Choose OML-P when:
• You want to support small creators for free
• You want compensation when projects succeed commercially
• You want your music accessible but reserve commercial rights

Choose OML-C when:
• You want immediate compensation for commercial use
• You're licensing to professional artists or businesses
• You want negotiated payment terms

Choose OML-S when:
• Your music will be used in visual media
• You want project-specific licensing
• You want to control scope of synchronization rights

AS A USER:

Use OML-F works when:
• You want maximum freedom with no restrictions
• You plan to redistribute or create sample packs
• You need access to stems and individual elements
• You want to build upon the work without limitations

Use OML-P works when:
• Your project revenue will stay under $1,000/year
• You're a hobbyist, student, or emerging creator
• You want free music with proper attribution

Use OML-C when:
• Your project will exceed $1,000/year revenue
• You need unrestricted commercial rights
• You're a professional artist or business

Use OML-S when:
• You're creating film, TV, advertising, or games
• You need synchronization rights for visual media
• You want defined scope and terms

KEY FEATURES

ATTRIBUTION MADE EASY
• No license in title required
• Include attribution anywhere accessible
• Metadata, descriptions, credits, documentation
• Flexible formats following industry standards

COMPATIBLE WITH SAMPLE LIBRARIES
• OML-P, OML-C, OML-S work with Splice, Loopcloud, etc.
• Clear distinction between finished work and underlying samples
• Respects original sample library terms
• OML-F requires fully original or open source materials

NEGOTIATED PRICING
• OML-C: Agree on royalty rate or flat fee
• OML-S: Negotiate sync fee based on project
• No rigid price ranges
• Real-world flexibility

SIMPLE AND CLEAR
• Plain English throughout
• "Can do" and "cannot do" sections
• Practical examples
• Easy to understand and implement

GETTING STARTED

FOR CREATORS:

1. Choose which license(s) to offer with your music
2. Include the license file(s) with your downloads
3. Add license info to file metadata
4. Provide contact information for inquiries
5. For OML-C and OML-S, be ready to negotiate terms

FOR USERS:

1. Check which OML license applies
2. Read the license terms
3. Provide proper attribution
4. For OML-P: Track revenue and upgrade to OML-C at $1K
5. For OML-C/OML-S: Contact creator to negotiate terms

PHILOSOPHY

The Open Music License framework exists to:

• Support creators through fair compensation
• Remove barriers to creativity and collaboration
• Provide clear, practical licensing options
• Respect both creator rights and user freedom
• Enable the next generation of music creation

OML-F brings open source principles to music
OML-P supports emerging artists while reserving commercial rights
OML-C enables fair commercial exploitation
OML-S simplifies sync licensing for visual media

Together, these licenses serve the diverse needs of the modern music ecosystem.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: Can I sell music made with OML-F works?
A: Yes! OML-F is completely open. Sell, modify, redistribute freely.

Q: What if my YouTube channel hits $1,000 using OML-P music?
A: Contact the creator within 30 days to negotiate OML-C terms.

Q: Can I use OML-P beats made with Splice?
A: Yes! OML-P is compatible with sample libraries.

Q: Do I need to put the license in my song title?
A: No. Just include it somewhere accessible like metadata or description.

Q: What's better, OML-F or OML-P?
A: Depends on your goals. OML-F for maximum openness, OML-P if you want 
compensation for commercial success.

Q: Can I negotiate lower rates for OML-C?
A: Yes! All terms are negotiable between creator and user.

Q: Does OML-S include master and sync rights?
A: Yes! OML-S provides complete clearance in one agreement.
